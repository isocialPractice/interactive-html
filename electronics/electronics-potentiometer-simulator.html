How Potentiometers Work:

  • Variable Resistance: Moving the dial changes the electron flow distance
  • Three Terminals: Two end terminals and one center wiper terminal
  • Voltage Control: Controls output voltage from the center pin
  • Current Control: Can also limit current in circuit
  • Brightness Control: Dial right = less resistance = brighter LED
  • Voltage Divider: Creates variable voltage between 0V and supply voltage

Common Applications:

  • Volume controls in audio equipment
  • Brightness adjustments in lights and displays
  • Temperature control in heaters
  • Speed control in motors
  • Analog input sensors for microcontrollers
